# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Korean students, recent graduates, and career changers navigating Korea's national certification system. They arrive with a goal (land a job, switch fields, prove a skill) but face a fragmented, intimidating landscape of 1,100+ qualifications. They use this tool in focused sessions — researching options, comparing difficulty and pass rates, and trusting an AI recommendation to cut through the noise.

## Product Purpose

CertFinder aggregates Korea's national technical and professional certification data and layers a hybrid RAG-powered recommendation engine on top. Success looks like a user who arrives uncertain and leaves with a clear, confident path: the right certifications for their major, goals, and current profile, ranked and explained.

## Brand Personality

Reliable · Precise · Empowering. The product earns trust through data density handled with clarity, not through reassuring marketing copy. It respects the user's intelligence and time.

## Anti-references

- Government and public portal sites (Q-Net, HRD Korea): cluttered tables, low contrast, no visual hierarchy, 2000s-era structure
- Generic SaaS dashboards: shadcn/Tailwind out-of-the-box defaults, cream backgrounds, icon+heading+text card grids, nothing that couldn't be any B2B tool
- Gamification overload: conspicuous XP bars, confetti, badge spam — feels like Duolingo, not a professional career tool
- Neon / crypto aesthetics: glowing borders, heavy glassmorphism, cyberpunk palette — wrong emotional register entirely

The mandate is professional, premium, and visually distinct. It should not be identifiable as "another React/Tailwind app."

## Design Principles

1. **Data earns the trust.** Pass rates, difficulty scores, job outlook figures are the product's core value. Present them with authority — precise, legible, never decorative.
2. **Premium through restraint.** Distinction comes from considered spacing, type hierarchy, and color discipline — not from added decoration. Every element that doesn't earn its place should be removed.
3. **Actively resist the category reflex.** Korean job-search tools default to portal-dense or generic SaaS. Both are failure modes. Surface and hierarchy choices should feel intentional, not templated.
4. **AI serves, it doesn't perform.** The recommendation engine is infrastructure. Its outputs should feel like expert guidance, not a chatbot showing off.
5. **Korean-first legibility.** Typography, line height, and density must be tuned for Korean text, not borrowed from English-language design references.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigation, sufficient contrast, screen reader labels on interactive elements. Korean language throughout; no special accommodations beyond baseline at this time.
